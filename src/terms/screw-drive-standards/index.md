<section class="about">
  <h2>Screw Drive Standards</h2>
  <p>
    Many industrial and technical applications rely on hexalobular (Torx-based) screw drive systems to achieve stable torque transfer, reduce cam-out, 
    and ensure predictable assembly. However, these drive types are frequently mixed or mislabeled in catalogs and field documentation.
  </p>
  <p>
    This reference defines the core Torx family and its variants, providing clear terminology for use in product data, specification sheets, and 
    AI-driven interpretation across manufacturing and service contexts.
  </p>
</section>

<section class="why">
  <h2>Why standardize terminology?</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Improves communication between suppliers, technicians, and designers</li>
    <li>Reduces incorrect tool selection in field work</li>
    <li>Enables consistent, machine-readable product data</li>
    <li>Supports AI classification and variant reasoning</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>Included drive types</h2>
  <p>
    The term set currently defines:
  </p>

  <ul>
    <li><a href="/terms/screw-drive-standards/torx/">Torx</a> — Standard hexalobular internal drive</li>
    <li><a href="/terms/screw-drive-standards/torx-security/">Security Torx (TR)</a> — Torx with center pin</li>
    <li><a href="/terms/screw-drive-standards/torx-plus/">Torx Plus (IP)</a> — Modified lobe geometry for higher contact area</li>
    <li><a href="/terms/screw-drive-standards/external-torx/">External Torx (E-Torx)</a> — External hexalobular profile for socket tools</li>
    <li><a href="/terms/screw-drive-standards/micro-torx/">Micro Torx</a> — Small profiles for electronics and precision assemblies</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>How to use these definitions</h2>
  <p>
    Each definition provides:
  </p>
  <ul>
    <li><a href="/terms/tool-impact-class/">Tool Impact Class</a> — Select bits based on fastening environment</li>
    <li>English, Swedish, and Brazilian Portuguese terminology</li>
    <li>Clear description of geometry and intended use</li>
    <li>Variant relationships (e.g., Torx Plus → Torx)</li>
    <li>Compatibility notes where relevant</li>
  </ul>
  <p>
    These definitions may be referenced directly in product tables, technical documentation, 
    CAD/BOM exports, and AI processing layers.
  </p>
</section>
